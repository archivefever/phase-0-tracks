2017 UK Snap Election Results

Overview

In a stunning result, the Conservative Party, led by Theresa May, has lost has a governing majority, winning only 318 seats, short of the 326 needed for control of Parliament.

Three important outcomes:

  1. There is a hung Parliament.
  2. May will strike a deal to form a minority government with the Democratic Unionist Party (DUP) of Northern Ireland.
  3. Jeremy Corbyn's Labour Party gained 29 seats, but is still 65 short of having its own majority.

What we still don't know:

  • What will DUP demand in return for its cooperation with the Conservative minority?
  • Will Labour's gains (and the Tories' loss) be interpreted as referendum on Brexit?
  • Does the poor showing of third-party candidates indicate that the recent trend away from two-party rule has crested?
